About

Saint Louis School of Aurora Hill, Inc.first opened its door for a course to the youth in June 1968. It was established to complement and enhance the church's mission of evangelization through the quality Christian education.Located at the building of Immaculate Conception Church, the school occupies the three lowers floors of the building. The church was inaugurated though in August 1965.

June 1968:The school was formally opened, with Rev.Fr Albert Van Pelt as the school director and parish priest with Sr.Carmel Fianzza as the first school princical. The initial enrollees for first year were 23 boys and girls. On its second year there were 34 first years and 28 second years. On the third year, Miss Felomena Asejo became the principal of the 18 first years, 31 second years and 31 third years.

The school gained its Government Recognition as a Secondary Education Institution from DECS in school year 1971-1972 with Mrs. Nellie Macadangdan-Olairex(Now Atty.Nellie M. Olairez) as the new principal of 41 first years, 27 second years, 43 third years and 37 fourth years. Of the 37 fourth years , 35 became a pioneer graduates of the school. The subsequent years saw the increase in student population and changes of the administration.

1976-1980:Rev.Fr Sotero Gayawet, as the School Director. He tailored improvements in the classrooms and the library of the school.

1981-1984: Rev. Fr.Patriciio Guyguyon, as the School Director. During his time, the school facilities were renewed, new cite for flag ceremony was installed within the school premises, cemented blocks on the church's frontage too, was built.

1985-1998: Rev. Fr. Pedro Rulloda, the school director then, instigated the improvement of the comfort rooms and setting up the basement accomodating two classrooms. No one will forget the killer earthquake that hit Baguio in July 16, 1990, which caused major damages to many school buildings. However, the strong foundation of our church and school building was able to withstand the tragedy with minor damages and classes were resumed on September on the same year.

1998-2003: Rev. Fr, Manny A. Castro. JCd was the school Director. Great improvement on the physical set up of the school was seen. It was during his time that the school playground was cemented and the spacios comfort rooms for boys and girs were constructed. There were renovations and great improvements wit the offices, classrooms, and the Computer Laboratory. All these improvements of the physical plants were realized through the effort of the School director.

Furthermore in 2000-2001, SLSAH placed first in Religion Quiz Bee, sponsored by tge Knights Of Columbos of Baguio, Second in 2001-2002 in the same event. In "CHEMAPHY" quiz bee sponsored by AMA Computer College of Baguio, SLSAH ranked fifth place of the 15 participating schools, from both public and private. Our athletes had also shown their physical fitness and sportmanship during interschool competitions, City District meets and others. They garnered victories and at the same time defeat makes them grow and mature as a person.

2003-2006: Rev. Fr Camilio Ongsit, the school director initiated the stone walling of the school ground and started the construction of the road from the church to the school grounds.

2006-2009: Rev. Fr. Seraph Balmadres, the school director completed the project started by Fr. Camilio Ongsit.

2009-2013: Rev. Fr. Jose Alipio, the school director initiated the construction driveway, and the Grotto. He initiated renovation of the offices to be more accesible and presentable, and carried out barangay masses. Fr. Jay passed away in October 2013, few months from end of his term as the Parish priest and School Director. There having no parish priest and school director, Rev. Fr. Andres M.Cosalan Jr. was assigned as Parochial and school administrator from May 2014 to April 2015.

May 2, 2015- January 2, 2017: Rev. Fr. Joseph B. Paduyao, the school director initiated the rehabilitiation of the school building by re-painting and re-installation of the gutters and downsprout.

Effective January 3 ,2017, the School Director is Rev. Fr. Matthew F.Dontogan III.

In school 2011-2012, SLSAH was awarded the "Best Diocesan School " among 16 schools. On the same school year, our school placed number one in the Luzon-wide search fir the Ten Outstanding Sons and Daughters of Migrant workers through our student Levy Lloyd Orcales.

All these and more proved that SLSAH has consistenly adhered to commitment of a Catholic School. But there is still greed need for improvement to cope up with the jet-age with high technology and globalization. With the concerted effort in the Administration, Faculty and Parents, SLSAH will continue to nurture a life transforming and Christ-disciple forming Catholic Educational community.

Saint Louis School of Aurora Hill, Inc. is of the Diocesan Schools uner the Diocese of Baguio. The sister schools aare Don Bosco School of Baguio City: Saint Louis School of Antamok, Itogon: Saint Louis School of Balatoc; Sacred Heart High School of Itogon; Saint Louis School of Sablan; St. Louis School-Philex; St. Theresita's School of Kapangan; St. Paul's Academy - Sayangan; San Isidro School of Abatan, and Immaculate Conception School of Bokod. Originally, these vicariate schools were run and managed by the Belgian CICM priests and ICM sisters from the early 920's up to the late 1970's when the Diocesan priests and SIHM sisters took over.
